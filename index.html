About Tina

My name is Tina Bizaca and I'm passionate about sharing ideas and communicating visually. I believe simple is not stupid and complexity doesn't need to be confusing. I am passionate about good experiences.

I'm a visual communicator

No matter the title, my primary role is always that of a visual communicator. From good visual communication comes clarity of complexity. When large data and intricate concepts can be condensed into big picture summaries, these ideas are transformed into accessible information. Only once the big picture is understood can the details be analyzed.

My goal is to always grasp the overall idea or problem and transfer the knowledge to others easily so a conversation can be encouraged:

My process

1. Problem

2. Research

  • Understand the company or client's background and process
  • Who is involved? Who do I need to talk to/interview?
    • Who am I solving the problem for?
    • Talk to stakeholders, project managers, support and marketing teams about what they know about the users
    • Research users by conducting user surveys or short interviews
    • Conduct competitive analysis if aim is to improve what others are already doing
    • View available usage analytics if applicable

3. What am I inheriting?

Is there an existing user-base or solution to this that users are familiar with?

4. Wireframe the flow

5. Review with shareholders, PMs, and other relevant team members

6. Reiterate steps 4-5 until a solution is agreed on

7. Review final mockups

8. Dev pairing

Work closely with the development team to integrate the designs in the development process by working with developers directly and coding the final designs

  • Use HTML, CSS/SCSS, templating systems such as Foundation or Bootstrap, JS, jQuery to get the job done
  • Review other work done across other products if necessary to ensure seamless and harmonized experiences and designs
